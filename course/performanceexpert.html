THE ACTUAL PROBLEM

Strategy doesn't matter if the data underneath it is lying to you.

Most marketing education skips straight to "how to run better ads" or "how to grow your blog." That advice assumes your tracking is accurate. For most businesses, it isn't — and nobody ever goes back to check.

What courses teach

The exciting layer

Ad copy, targeting, content calendars, "growth hacks." All built on top of an assumption nobody verifies.

What's actually broken

The plumbing underneath

GA4 not tracking real conversions. Pixel firing on the wrong event. Search Console never verified. Nobody touches Tag Manager because nobody understands the container.

The result

Strategy built on noise

You optimize toward numbers that were never measuring the right thing. Every decision downstream inherits the error.

THE SIX, WIRED TOGETHER

It's not six separate tools. It's one data pipe with six inlets.

Each module teaches the "why" before the click-path — what breaks downstream if you get it wrong, and when a tool (like Tag Manager) genuinely isn't the right answer at all.

01

Google Analytics 4

events · conversions · multi-currency · audiences

Not "install the snippet." Event architecture that survives coupons, multiple payment rails, and currency switches without quietly corrupting your revenue numbers — the exact mess most real checkouts produce and most tutorials never show.

WHY IT BREAKSEvents fire before discounts apply. Nobody catches it.
02

Search Console

verification · indexing · query data · core web vitals

Verifying ownership properly, reading what Google actually thinks of your site, and using query data to inform content instead of guessing.

WHY IT BREAKSVerified once, never opened again.
03

Tag Manager — and when to skip it

containers · triggers · when gtag alone is cleaner

GTM is taught as mandatory scaffolding everywhere. It isn't. You'll learn to judge when wiring GA4/Ads directly is the more maintainable choice — and when a container actually earns its complexity.

WHY IT BREAKSContainers nobody understands become containers nobody touches.
04

Merchant Center

product feed · approval · disapproval triage

Getting a product feed actually approved, and diagnosing the disapproval reasons that send most store owners into a support-ticket spiral.

WHY IT BREAKSOne feed attribute error halts every Shopping ad silently.
05

Meta Pixel

standard events · custom conversions · custom-coded sites

Straightforward on supported platforms — the part nobody teaches is wiring it correctly on a fully custom-coded site, where there's no app to "just connect."

WHY IT BREAKSFires on page load instead of the real event.
06

AdSense for blogs

approval · placement · policy compliance

Getting approved without the common rejection traps, and placing units where they earn without wrecking the reading experience or violating policy.

WHY IT BREAKSApproved once, then silently de-monetized for a policy drift nobody noticed.
