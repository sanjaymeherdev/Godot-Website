FAQ

Questions before you buy.

The things most people ask before messaging.

Is this the official WhatsApp API?
LeadFlow uses an unofficial WhatsApp connection — the same method as WhatsApp Web. It is not the official Meta Business API. This means lower cost and no template approvals, but also means Meta could detect and temporarily block mass-sending if you push too hard too fast. The throttle controls are there for a reason — use them. For high-volume official API usage, ask about the Wablast product instead.
Do I need to keep my phone connected?
Yes — just like WhatsApp Web, your phone needs to stay connected to the internet while sending. You don't need to have WhatsApp open on your phone, but the phone must be online. Most users leave it on Wi-Fi and charging during campaigns.
Where is my data stored?
Your lead and contact data is stored in a Supabase database. It's shared infrastructure managed by Sanjay — but each account's data is isolated. If you want fully private infrastructure, ask about deploying your own instance.
How is this different from WATI or AiSensy?
WATI and AiSensy use the official Meta API — more compliant, but costs ₹3,000–6,000/month minimum regardless of how many messages you send. LeadFlow is pay-per-message at ₹0.25 with no monthly platform fee. For most small businesses sending under 10,000 messages/month, LeadFlow is significantly cheaper.
Can I use my existing WhatsApp number?
Yes. You link whatever WhatsApp number you want — personal or business. Just scan the QR code and you're connected. You can unlink anytime. Using the number on LeadFlow doesn't affect your regular WhatsApp usage.
What happens if I run out of credits mid-campaign?
The campaign pauses automatically and shows a warning. No messages are attempted with zero credits — you're never charged for failed sends. Top up on WhatsApp and resume from where it stopped.
